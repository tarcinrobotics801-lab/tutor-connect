# Tarcin MBA Internship - Student Application and Onboarding Flow

## Purpose

This document captures the student-side process for the MBA internship campaign. It covers how students move from application to WhatsApp group, orientation, selection, and onboarding.

This is a review draft and can be updated after team discussion.

---

## Overall Student Funnel

```text
Flyer / College Share / WhatsApp Message
        ↓
Google Form Application
        ↓
Form Confirmation Message with WhatsApp Group Link
        ↓
Student Joins WhatsApp Group
        ↓
Group Updates + Orientation
        ↓
Role Preference Confirmation
        ↓
Shortlisting
        ↓
Selection
        ↓
Onboarding
        ↓
First-Week Task Assignment
```

---

## Core Principle

The Google Form should not only collect student data. It should act as the entry point into the student communication funnel.

Instead of manually messaging every applicant, students should be guided to join the official WhatsApp group immediately after submitting the form.

This reduces manual follow-up and helps the team communicate updates, orientation details, role expectations, and next steps in one place.

## Orientation and Selection SOP

To move students from "applied and joined WhatsApp group" to "selected and onboarded" in a structured way.

### Stage 1 - Student Joins WhatsApp Group

- Student has filled Google Form

## Stage 3 - Orientation Agenda

Recommended duration: 30 to 45 minutes

| Section | Duration | Content |
|---|---:|---|
| Welcome and context | 5 minutes | Introduce Tarcin as a product development company building platforms across robotics, EdTech, automation, and digital learning systems |
| Internship pathways | 5 minutes | Explain short-term internship and 6-month AICTE-listed pathway |
| Role areas | 10 minutes | Explain Marketing & Outreach, Digital Marketing, Business Analytics, Customer Engagement, Operations & Program Coordination |
| What students will do | 10 minutes | Explain practical tasks such as campaign support, CRM workflows, student engagement, data tracking, documentation, and program coordination |
| Expectations | 5 minutes | Explain participation, communication, weekly task completion, professionalism, and no certificate-only mindset |
| Selection and onboarding | 5 minutes | Explain form review, attendance, role preference confirmation, shortlisting, and onboarding |
| Questions | 5 to 10 minutes | Take common questions |

## Role-Wise First Week Task Plan

### Purpose

To give every selected intern a clear first-week structure so they start contributing immediately and the team can evaluate seriousness, communication, and role fit.

### Common First-Week Expectations For All Interns

All interns must:
- Attend onboarding/orientation
- Join assigned communication channel
- Confirm role, reporting person, and availability
- Submit daily updates
- Complete first-week task by deadline
- Ask questions early, not at the last minute

### Daily Reporting Format

```text
Daily Update - [DATE]

Name:
Role:
Tasks completed today:
Pending tasks:
Support needed:
Plan for tomorrow:
```

### Weekly Review Criteria

Each intern is reviewed on:
- Timely communication
- Clarity of work
- Completion of assigned task
- Initiative
- Role fit
- Professional attitude

## First Week Plan - Marketing & Outreach Intern

### First-Week Objective

Understand Tarcin's internship/product positioning and prepare a usable outreach base for colleges, student communities, or partner channels.

### Day 1

- Read Tarcin overview and internship pathways
- Understand target audience: MBA students, TPOs, college coordinators
- Review sample outreach messages

### Day 2-3

Prepare a list of 20 potential outreach channels:
- Colleges
- MBA departments
- Student clubs
- Placement cells
- LinkedIn student groups
- Alumni contacts
- Local communities

For each entry, capture:
- Name
- Contact person, if available
- Email / phone / link
- Location
- Why this is relevant
- Priority: High / Medium / Low

### Day 4-5

Draft one outreach message for any one segment:
- College TPO
- Student group
- Campus ambassador
- LinkedIn outreach

### Expected Output

- Outreach prospect list with 20 entries
- One draft outreach message
- Short note: top 5 priority contacts and why

### Review Criteria

- Relevance of contacts
- Completeness of information
- Practical thinking
- Quality of message

## Suggested First-Week Decisions

- Continue in assigned role
- Reassign to better-fit role
- Move to observation for one more week
- Drop due to no response or inactivity
- Consider for extended responsibility if strong performer
